I can't hear music when using Mighty with Bose wired headphones.

We have released a new software update (v1.0) that supports Bose wired headphones. Please follow these steps to install:

Connect your Mighty to the app via Bluetooth

I can't hear music when using Mighty with my Bose SoundSport wireless headphones.

There's a known compatibility issue between older version of the Mighty firmware and the Bose SoundSport wireless headphones (If you have SoundSport Free, please see note at the bottom).

GOOD TO KNOW
This issue was resolved with our recent firmware release (v1.0). To update to the latest firmware, connect Mighty to the app, give it a WiFi connection, navigate to the User tab (bottom right), and click "Software Update". To check which version of the Mighty firmware that you're running, click "About" and look at the number listed below "Mighty Version".

Bose SoundSport Free
Unfortunately, Mighty isn't currently compatible with the Bose SoundSport Free headphones. Those headphones use a different Bluetooth protocol for music playback that we've yet to integrate into Mighty's software. The good news is that we can add support for the SoundSport Free via an over-the-air software update that will be installed through the Mighty app. We're working on a fix right now and hope to have something released in the Spring of 2018.

I hear a voice saying "Spotify login failed" when I try to play music on Mighty.

Please try the following steps to re-establish your Spotify login in the Mighty:

- Connect your Mighty back to your mobile app
- Connect the Mighty to WiFi
- Logout of Spotify within the Mighty app
- Then log back into Spotify within the Mighty app
- Check to see if your music plays
- If not, reconnect it back to the mobile app and establish WiFi
- Then click ""Refresh the playlists on your Mighty"" found on the Music tab (bottom center tab)

The music skips when I use Mighty with a Bluetooth accessory.

This is a common Bluetooth issue, especially for small devices like Mighty. The Bluetooth antenna in Mighty is smaller than the antenna in most smartphones, so it's more difficult to maintain a strong Bluetooth connection. We've found that playback is most stable under these conditions:

- Restart Mighty prior to playback. To power off, press and hold the play/pause button until the LED goes orange. Wait twenty seconds, then press the play/pause button to power Mighty back on. Mighty is fully powered on once the LED goes from solid green to blinking green (if not plugged into charger) or white (if plugged into charger).
- Wear Mighty outward facing on your sleeve, pocket, or wristband.
- If playback starts to get interrupted, put your Mighty right next to the headphones for a few seconds and it should stabilize.
- If playback starts to get interrupted, don't press any buttons for a few seconds. Just let the issue resolve itself.
- Use the Normal Download Quality when syncing your Spotify playlists. This setting can be adjusted in the Connections tab (bottom left) by clicking on the Spotify logo.

GOOD TO KNOW
We're working on a software update that will improve the playback experience. We hope to release the software update in December 2017.

The volume is low when I use Mighty with a Bluetooth accessory.

If your Bluetooth accessory has onboard controls, press the volume up button until the volume is as loud as possible. Then, press the volume up button on Mighty until the volume is as loud as possible. You will then be listening at the maximum possible volume.

Connecting Mighty to App

How do I connect my Mighty to the mobile app?

Navigate to the Connections tab (bottom left) and click on the Bluetooth icon to see all Mightys in your proximity. Click the plus button (“+”) next to your Mighty to connect.

GOOD TO KNOW
If Mighty’s LED blinks blue, you need to press the play/pause button on the Mighty in order to establish the connection. (Note: The blue LED only occurs in certain versions of the Mighty)

How do I disconnect my Mighty from the app?

Navigate to the Connections tab (bottom left) and click on the Bluetooth icon at the top of the screen to see your Mighty. You’ll see a blue checkmark to the left of your Mighty’s name to indicate that it's connected to the mobile app. Swipe left on your Mighty’s name to display the disconnection icon, then tap the icon to disconnect your Mighty from the app.

My Mighty isn't showing up in the app. What should I do?

First, make sure that your Mighty is plugged into a charger. The LED should turn green for thirty seconds while Mighty powers on, then should turn white to indicate that it's charging. If the LED doesn't turn any color or is stuck on solid green, please email us at heyo@bemighty.com and we'll respond within one business day to help you troubleshoot.

If the LED is white and your Mighty still isn't showing up in the app, please try the following:

- Restart Mighty. To power off, press and hold the play/pause button until the LED goes orange. Wait twenty seconds, then press the play/pause button to power Mighty back on. Mighty is fully powered on once the LED goes from solid green to blinking green (if not plugged into charger) or white (if plugged into charger).
- Android users only: Turn off your phone's Location services and Bluetooth services, wait 5 seconds, then turn them back on. You may need to do this through the Settings on your phone as opposed to the Quick Access bar. Then, open the Mighty mobile app and try to connect your Mighty again. If prompted, you must give Mighty access to your location (this is an Android specific Bluetooth Low Energy requirement).

If the above steps do not resolve the issue, please email us at heyo@bemighty.com and we'll respond within one business day to help you troubleshoot.

2. Restart Mighty. To power off, press and hold the play/pause button until the LED goes orange. Wait twenty seconds, then press the play/pause button to power Mighty back on. Mighty is fully powered on once the LED goes from solid green to blinking green (if not plugged into charger) or white (if plugged into charger).
3. Close and re-open the mobile app.
4. - Android users only: Turn off your phone's Location services and Bluetooth services, wait 5 seconds, then turn them back on. You may need to do this through the Settings on your phone as opposed to the Quick Access bar. Then, open the Mighty mobile app and try to connect your Mighty again. If prompted, you must give Mighty access to your location (this is an Android specific Bluetooth Low Energy requirement).

If the steps above do not resolve the issue, please email us at heyo@bemighty.com and we'll respond within one business day to help you troubleshoot.

Connecting Mighty to a Bluetooth accessory

Does my Mighty work with all Bluetooth accessories (e.g. headphones and speakers)?

Mighty works with the vast majority of Bluetooth accessories. If Mighty's not working with your Bluetooth accessory, please read through this FAQ for more information on connecting Mighty to Bluetooth accessories. If you still can't resolve the issue, please email us at heyo@bemighty.com and we'll respond within one business day to help you troubleshoot.

How do I connect my Mighty to a Bluetooth accessory for the first time?

1. Put your Bluetooth accessory in pairing mode, which is generally denoted by a blinking blue and/or red LED (this may be different than just turning on the accessory)
2. Connect Mighty to the mobile app, navigate to the Connections tab (bottom left), click the Bluetooth logo at the top of the screen, and pull down on the screen to search for all Bluetooth accessories in your proximity.
3. Press the plus (“+”) button next to the name of the Bluetooth accessory to pair and connect it with your Mighty. A blue checkmark will be displayed to the left of the Bluetooth accessory name and you will hear a two-tone beep through the Bluetooth accessory to indicate that the connection was successful. Press the play/pause button on your Mighty to initiate playback through the Bluetooth accessory.

GOOD TO KNOW
Mighty does not require a PIN to establish a connection with Bluetooth accessories

How do I reconnect my Mighty to a Bluetooth accessory?

Mighty will automatically reconnect to any previously paired Bluetooth accessory in your proximity. If Mighty doesn't automatically reconnect to the Bluetooth accessory, please follow these steps:

1. Start with Mighty and the Bluetooth accessory powered off.
2. Power on Mighty and wait for the LED to blink green, indicating that it’s powered on.
3. Power on the Bluetooth accessory.
4. You will hear a two-tone beep through the Bluetooth accessory to indicate that the reconnection was successful.

If you've followed the steps above and still cannot reconnect to the Bluetooth accessory, please email us at heyo@bemighty.com and we'll respond within one business day to help you troubleshoot.

How do I disconnect my Mighty from a Bluetooth accessory?

Connect Mighty to the app, navigate to the Connections tab (bottom left), and click the Bluetooth logo at the top of the screen. A bluecheck will be displayed to the left of the Bluetooth accessory that is currently connected to Mighty. Swipe left on the Bluetooth accessory to display an unlinked chain icon, then tap the unlinked chain icon to disconnect it from Mighty.

Can I pair my Mighty with more than one Bluetooth accessory?

Yes, you can pair Mighty to multiple accessories. All previously paired Bluetooth accessories will be listed in the Bluetooth section of the app.

My Bluetooth accessory isn't showing up when I search for it in the app. What should I do?

1. Remember to swipe down on the Bluetooth screen to initiate a search for Bluetooth accessories.
2. Make sure the Bluetooth accessory is in pairing mode (this may be different than just turning on the accessory)
3. Confirm that the Bluetooth accessory isn't actively connected to another device (e.g. your phone). If so, disconnect it first prior to searching for the accessory in the Mighty mobile app.

If you've followed the steps above and still cannot find the Bluetooth accessory in the app, please email us at heyo@bemighty.com and we'll respond within one business day to help you troubleshoot.

I see a Bluetooth accessory listed as "n/a" in the app. What should I do?

In certain situations, Bluetooth accessories that have not been previously paired with Mighty will be listed as “n/a” in the mobile app. This is due to a delay in passing the make/model information from the Bluetooth accessory to the mobile app. You can still connect to the Bluetooth accessory and the make/model information will automatically be displayed in the app once the information is available.

I get a "connection failed" error when I try to connect my Mighty to a Bluetooth accessory. What should I do?

1. Restart Mighty and try again. To power off, press and hold the play/pause button until the LED goes orange. Wait twenty seconds, then press the play/pause button to power Mighty back on. Mighty is fully powered on once the LED goes from solid green to blinking green (if not plugged into charger) or white (if plugged into charger).
2. Restart the Bluetooth accessory and try to connect to it again.

If you've followed the steps above and still receive an error message, please email us at heyo@bemighty.com and we'll respond within one business day to help you troubleshoot.

I connected my Mighty to a Bluetooth accessory once but it won't reconnect. What should I do?

1. Start with Mighty and the Bluetooth accessory powered off.
2. Power on Mighty and wait for the LED to blink green, indicating that it’s powered on.
3. Power on the Bluetooth accessory.
4. You will hear a two-tone beep through the Bluetooth accessory to indicate that the reconnection was successful.

If you've followed the steps above and still cannot reconnect to the Bluetooth accessory, please email us at heyo@bemighty.com and we'll respond within one business day to help you troubleshoot.

Does Mighty work with AirPods?

Yes, Mighty works with AirPods. Please make sure that the AirPods are in pairing mode and are disconnected from your phone prior to searching for them in the Mighty app.

Will Mighty connect to my car's Bluetooth system?

Mighty works with most Bluetooth-enabled car entertainment systems. If you’re unable to find your car entertainment system in the Mighty app, you may need to make it “visible” to Mighty. Check your car’s user manual for information on making the Bluetooth system visible (also referred to as “discoverable”).

No, most Bluetooth accessories can only be actively connected to one device at a time. This is a Bluetooth limitaition that Mighty does not control. Please make sure that the Bluetooth accessory isn't actively connected to another device (e.g. your phone) prior to searching for the accessory in the Mighty app.

Your Spotify Account

How do I connect my Spotify account to Mighty?

We recommend that you follow the Mighty Setup Guide. Power on your Mighty, plug it into a charger, and launch the mobile app. Click ""Setup"" and follow the on-screen instructions. You will be guided through the Spotify account connection process.

You can manually connect your Spotify account to Mighty by clicking ""No Thanks"" on the Mighty Setup Guide page. Navigate to the Connections tab (bottom left) and click on the Spotify icon at the top of the screen, then click ""Connect"" to connect your Spotify account to Mighty. You will be redirected to Spotify establish the connection, then redirected back to the Mighty app. A green checkmark will be displayed next to the Spotify icon to indicate that the connection was successful.

GOOD TO KNOW
You must have a Spotify Premium account in order to take your playlists offline with Mighty. If you don’t have a Spotify Premium account, you can get one at www.spotify.com/premium.

How do I disconnect my Spotify account from Mighty?

Navigate to the Connections tab (bottom left), click on the Spotify icon at the top of the screen, then click "Logout".

How do I switch the Spotify account that's connected to Mighty?

Open the Spotify app on your phone and click "Settings" in the upper right-hand corner of the app. Click "Logout", then follow the on-screen instructions to login to the desired Spotify account. If you do not have the Spotify app installed on your phone, open and web browser and go to spotify.com to sign into the account that you would like to connect to Mighty. Then navigate to the Connections tab (bottom left) and click on the Spotify icon at the top of the screen, then click "Connect" to connect your Spotify account to Mighty. You will be redirected to Spotify establish the connection, then redirected back to the Mighty app.

I get a "connection failed" or "No Internet connect" error when I try to connect my Spotify account to Mighty. What should I do?

We leverage the native Spotify mobile app (or the web-based login) to log you into Spotify. Please try to delete both the native Spotify app and the Mighty app from your phone, then re-download both of them. Then, launch the Mighty app and sign in through Spotify through the app.

GOOD TO KNOW
There may be some issues if you have reached your 3 offline device limit on your Spotify account. Please login to your Spotify account to check and if you reached your maximum 3 offline devices, to remove one and then try again.

The app freezes when I try to connect my Spotify account to Mighty. What should I do?

We leverage the native Spotify mobile app (or the web-based login) to log you into Spotify. Please try to delete both the native Spotify app and the Mighty app from your phone, then re-download both of them. Then, launch the Mighty app and sign in through Spotify through the app.

Does Mighty work with Spotify for Family (Premium) accounts?

Yes, Mighty works with Spotify for Family.

Yes, Mighty works in every country where Spotify Premium is available.

The download never completes when I try to update my Mighty's firmware. What should I do?

We are aware of some issues between the Mighty device and certain WiFi routers. Generally, most of users who are experiencing what you have been experiencing have found success by using their phones as a hotspot. Obviously, this is not an ideal solution, but possible to help for the short term until you get the update. Note: If you're an iPhone user there's a known limitation on certain iPhones where you need to connect another device to the hotspot before it will be visible in the Mighty app.

Alternatively, you can try the following suggestions to adjust the settings on your WiFi router and trying again.
Change your router from 2.4 Ghz to 5 Ghz
Change to a different channel # while on 2.4 Ghz
Move the Mighty to about 1.5 to 2 meters away from the router and keep it around the same height as your router (For example, if your router is sitting on a table with the antennas vertical pointing up, the mighty should also be on the table about 1.5 to 2 meters away).

I'm giving my Mighty to someone else. What should I do before giving it away?

We require every Mighty to be registered to a specific user as a security measure. If you would like to give your Mighty someone else, you will need to de-register your Mighty. Here's how you de-register a Mighty from one user and register it with another user. Let's assume that the Mighty has been registered by User 1 and you'd like to give the Mighty to User 2.

1. Sign into the Mighty app with User 1.
2. Connect to the Mighty over Bluetooth.
3. Navigate to the User tab (bottom right) and click Logout. Important: This must be done while the Mighty is connected over Bluetooth.
4. Sign back into the Mighty app with User 2.
5. Connect to the Mighty over Bluetooth. You will receive a popup saying that existing playlists will be removed, click to continue.
6. The Mighty will be registered to User 2 moving forward.
